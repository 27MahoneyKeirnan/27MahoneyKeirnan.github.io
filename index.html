According to [cite encyclopedia brittanica], "They, like the house centipedes, run with the body held straight and are the fastest moving centipedes." Centipedes, of the class Chilopoda, any of various long, many-segmented predaceous arthropods, with flattened bodies. Each of the segments of the centipede (minus the hindmost) bears a single pair of legs. During the day, these creatures will hide under bark, stones, or any other objects they can skitter underneath. This is because they prefer damp habitats. At night, however, they roam the lands in a hunt for small invertebrates. Fast Facts: -They move quickly on their legs, which may contain 14-177 pairs, with one pair of many-jointed antennae armed with jaw-like venomus claws not far behind the head -The legs grow longer at the back, while they are rather short towards the front. The final two pairs, hoerver, are the longest, and are used in mating and the capture of prey -There are over 20 known species in Australia -They can range from 4-40 millimeters in length -They eat fungal spores, decaying plants, and on the soft parts os mall insects, slaters and spiders. They are omivores -They can be purple-ish or red-brown in color -Their bodies contain 15 segments with one pair of legs per segment.
